OPTICS doesn't measure
agile maturity.

It identifies system risk in Agile teams — the kind that shows up as cost, attrition, or late delivery before anyone noticed anything was wrong.

Observing Performance · Teams · Interactions · Capabilities · in Systems SM · PO · TL · EM · Coach

The team is the system. The system includes people, processes, artifacts, backlogs, stakeholders, and relationships with other teams. OPTICS observes that system through Dimensions and Aspects, then produces a diagnostic risk profile.

The knowledge model

A score below 50% is a risk signal, not a verdict. It may be transient, context-dependent, or a false positive. The report language reflects that — "this pattern is consistent with..." — never "your team fails at X."

Trajectory

Each completed assessment stamps a score and date. The team screen shows the score history, delta from previous, and a trajectory sparkline. A team going 62→58→51 tells a different story than 51→58→62.

Defensibility

Every risk has a source.
Not intuition — literature.

The theoreticalAnchor field is mandatory on every risk entry. If you can't cite a source, you hedge the language or don't ship the risk.

Edmondson (1999, 2018) Psychological safety, team learning — PR-001, DR-005
Deci & Ryan · Pink Drive Autonomy & growth — PR-002, attrition risk
Hackman (2002) Team composition, shared mental models — PR-003, stability risks
DORA / Accelerate (2018) Flow metrics — DR-010, future Engineering DLC
Scrum Guide 2020 Daily sync, planning, commitment, retrospection, DoD — DR-001 to DR-011
Cagan — Inspired · Perri — Escaping the Build Trap Product strategy, PO empowerment, outcome focus — PDR-001 to PDR-006
Team Topologies (Skelton & Pais 2019) External dependencies, team interfaces — DR-006, DR-008
Marquet — Turn the Ship Around Team autonomy, decision ownership — LR-001, LR-002, LR-004
